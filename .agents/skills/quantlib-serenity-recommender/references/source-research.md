# Source Research Rules

## Goal

Serenity recommendations must explain why a stock is a structural bottleneck
beneficiary. Price and factor data are only the double-check layer; the thesis
must be supported by source evidence.

## Source Priority

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Official company materials: investor presentations, annual reports, earnings
   call decks, shareholder meeting materials, and company announcements.
2. Regulatory and exchange sources: MOPS, TWSE/TPEx disclosures, monthly revenue
   filings, and financial statements.
3. Primary industry data: industry associations, supplier/customer disclosures,
   shipment/capacity data, and credible market research excerpts.
4. Reputable financial media with named facts, dates, and attributable sources.
5. Social media, forums, and anonymous commentary only as sentiment context, not
   as structural proof.

## Required Evidence Per Recommended Stock

For each top-10 recommendation, collect enough evidence to answer:

- What is the bottleneck?
- Why is demand likely persistent rather than one-off?
- Why does this company directly benefit?
- What evidence shows pricing power, volume leverage, qualification barriers, or
  capacity scarcity?
- What news or data would invalidate the thesis?

Include source title/name, publish date or filing date, and URL when available.
If current web evidence cannot be found, state the gap and lower confidence.

## Web Research Behavior

Use web search when the recommendation depends on current news, filings,
earnings calls, industry developments, or any claim not already supported by
fresh local project artifacts. Prefer direct official pages over reposts.

For webpages and articles, extract clean content where possible before
summarizing. Avoid long quotes. Summarize evidence in the report and link the
source.
